Tutor hourly rates (2026 guide)

Updated July 2026 · 6 min read

Tutoring rates span a huge range — a high-schooler helping with algebra and a certified SAT specialist can be 5× apart. This guide gives you realistic 2026 numbers by subject, level and format, the factors that move them, and how to keep a clean record if you pay a tutor every week.

The short answer

In 2026, private tutors in the US typically charge $25 to $80 per hour. The middle of the market — a qualified tutor for a K–12 subject — sits around $40–$60/hour. Casual peer or college-student tutors run cheaper; specialized test prep and advanced subjects run higher.

In-person vs. online

Online tutoring is usually cheaper — no travel time, and the tutor can serve more students. In-person tends to cost more and suits younger kids or hands-on subjects. If cost is the main constraint, online is the easy win; if focus and accountability matter more, in-person can be worth the premium.

How to land on a fair number

  1. Start from the middle of the market for the subject and level (~$40–$60/hour for most K–12).
  2. Adjust up for credentials, test prep or advanced subjects; down for a student tutor or online.
  3. Ask about a package rate if you'll book weekly for a term.
  4. Agree the hourly rate and session length up front, and track the hours so the monthly cost never surprises you.
